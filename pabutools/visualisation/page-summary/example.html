Results of the Civic Budget in Świecie in 2024

The election was used equal share methods. The available budget was PLN 1,070,000, and 2,553 residents voted correctly. For every voter. therefore it was around PLN 419 Due to the nature of the method (.interested parties are referred to a detailed description) this amount has increased up to PLN 661. In other words, each group of 100 voters could decide on a project whose cost did not exceed PLN 66,111. Of the submitted projects, 22 were positively verified from the formal side, of which. 17 selected for implementation.

The total cost of selected projects is PLN 1,040,337. Below is a list of submitted projects. Projects that have been selected for implementation have been marked in green. Projects that were rejected in the first phase were marked in red. These are projects that are too expensive in relation to the number of votes obtained. (The number of supporters for such a project divided by the number of all voters is less than the cost of the project divided by three times the value of the budget available.)

We give the cost and number of votes he received for each project. In addition, we provide him for each project effective support, which is the percentage describing to what extent the amount of support exceeds the threshold required for project acceptance: a value above 100% means that the project was selected and a value below 100% means that the project was not selected. For example, an effective support value of 60% means that the project received 60% of the votes required for acceptance.

Projekty mogły zostać niewybrane z jednego z następujących powodów:
  1. the project received too few votes in relation to its cost, or
  2. voters who voted for a given project also voted for other more popular projects, so their votes translated first to finance these more popular projects.

We also present with each project bar diagram. He explains what amount the supporters of each project initially had. In the diagrams we can also see why the project was not selected. In particular, we can see which more popular projects were voted by supporters of a given unselected project, and what part of their funds was allocated to these more popular projects.

Porównanie metody równych udziałów z metodą większościową

Przeprowadziliśmy analizę, porównując metodę równych udziałów do metody większościowej, która jest najczęsciej używaną metodą w budżetach obywatelskich.

  1. W przypadku metody równych udziałów 0,16% wyborców nie otrzymało żadnego z projektów, na który zagłosowali. Gdyby użyć metody większościowej, to odsetek ten wynosiłby 9,09%.
  2. W przypadku metody równych udziałów średnio dla wyborcy wybrano 2,47 projektów spośród tych na które zagłosował. Gdyby użyć metody większościowej, to liczba ta wynosiłaby 1,90.
